# ADR-003

## Title

Patterns Reveal Recurring Educational Structures

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Date

2026-07-14

---

## Context

Early Pattern discussions focused on grouping similar observations.

Further theoretical exploration demonstrated that similarity alone is insufficient.

Educational Reality contains unlimited variation.

The role of Pattern is therefore not classification, but revealing recurring educational structures that gradually become visible through accumulated observations.

---

## Decision

Patterns are defined as recurring educational structures emerging from multiple observations across time, context and perspectives.

Patterns do not create reality.

They reveal structures already present within Educational Reality.

---

## Rationale

This decision shifts Pattern recognition away from superficial similarity and toward educational coherence.

It also separates Pattern from Interpretation, Evidence and Insight.

Pattern increases awareness.

It does not generate meaning.

---

## Consequences

Pattern becomes an awareness layer.

Evidence will justify trust in Patterns.

Insight will interpret their educational meaning.

Pattern recognition remains descriptive and explainable.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Patterns are groups of similar observations.

Rejected because educational similarity does not necessarily represent educational structure.

### Patterns classify learners.

Rejected because Pattern exists to increase awareness rather than assign labels.
