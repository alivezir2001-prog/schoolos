# ADR-005

## Title

Evidence Justifies Trust

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Date

2026-07-14

---

## Context

As Pattern Theory evolved, it became clear that awareness alone is insufficient for responsible educational understanding.

Educators require observable reasons before trusting a Pattern.

This led to the definition of Evidence as a distinct theoretical layer.

---

## Decision

Evidence exists to justify trust in educational Patterns.

Evidence provides observable, traceable and explainable justification.

Evidence does not create Patterns.

Evidence does not interpret educational meaning.

---

## Rationale

Trust should always be supported by observable educational reasons.

Separating justification from interpretation increases transparency and explainability.

---

## Consequences

Evidence becomes the Justification Layer.

Confidence estimates the strength of available justification.

Insight interprets educational meaning only after Evidence exists.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Evidence generates educational meaning.

Rejected because meaning belongs to Insight Theory.

### Evidence estimates confidence.

Rejected because confidence is an independent responsibility.
