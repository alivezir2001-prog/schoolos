# ADR-002

## Title

Observation Preserves Reality

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Date

2026-07-14

---

## Context

Observation was initially considered a possible source of interpretation.

As the Observation Theory matured, it became clear that educational understanding requires a strict separation between observation and interpretation.

Mixing these responsibilities would reduce explainability and introduce subjective bias into the knowledge model.

---

## Decision

Observation exists solely to preserve authentic fragments of Educational Reality.

Observations must remain descriptive.

They never interpret, explain, classify or evaluate.

Meaning emerges only in later theoretical layers.

---

## Rationale

Separating observation from interpretation protects historical integrity.

Future understanding may change.

Past observations should not.

This distinction ensures that educational knowledge remains traceable and explainable.

---

## Consequences

Observation becomes immutable historical evidence.

Pattern recognition depends upon accumulated observations.

No AI component may modify the content of an observation after it has been recorded.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Observations may contain interpretation.

Rejected because interpretation belongs to later layers.

### Observations may be edited after new understanding emerges.

Rejected because historical educational reality must remain trustworthy.
