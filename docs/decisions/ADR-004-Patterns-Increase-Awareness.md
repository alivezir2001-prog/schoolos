# ADR-004

## Title

Patterns Increase Educational Awareness

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Date

2026-07-14

---

## Context

During the development of Pattern Theory, Patterns were initially viewed as mechanisms for grouping observations.

Further theoretical exploration demonstrated that the true purpose of a Pattern is not classification but awareness.

Educational action can only begin after educational reality becomes progressively visible.

---

## Decision

Patterns exist to increase educational awareness.

Their responsibility is to reveal recurring educational structures that remain difficult to notice when observations are viewed individually.

Patterns never generate meaning.

Patterns never make educational decisions.

---

## Rationale

Educational awareness always precedes educational understanding.

Without awareness, educators cannot intentionally support learner development.

Separating awareness from interpretation keeps the knowledge model explainable.

---

## Consequences

Pattern becomes the Awareness Layer of SchoolOS.

Evidence justifies trust in Patterns.

Insights interpret their educational meaning.

Patterns remain descriptive.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Patterns classify learners.

Rejected because classification limits educational understanding.

### Patterns generate educational meaning.

Rejected because meaning belongs to Insight Theory.
