# ADR-001

## Title

Educational Reality Precedes Observation

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Date

2026-07-14

---

## Context

During the early design of SchoolOS, observations were initially considered the starting point of educational knowledge.

Further theoretical work revealed that observations do not create educational reality.

Educational reality already exists independently of teachers, schools, observations, or artificial intelligence.

Observation merely provides access to fragments of that reality.

Recognizing this distinction became essential for building an explainable educational intelligence model.

---

## Decision

Educational Reality is defined as the ontological foundation of SchoolOS.

Educational Reality always exists before Observation.

Observation never creates reality.

It only captures authentic fragments of an already existing educational reality.

---

## Rationale

Grounding SchoolOS in Educational Reality prevents the system from confusing recorded information with reality itself.

This distinction establishes intellectual humility.

It also ensures that future AI capabilities assist awareness rather than claiming authority over reality.

---

## Consequences

All theoretical layers depend on Educational Reality.

Observation becomes a capture mechanism rather than a creator of knowledge.

Pattern, Evidence, Insight and Understanding ultimately refer back to Educational Reality.

Educational Reality therefore becomes the foundation of the entire knowledge model.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Observation creates educational knowledge.

Rejected because observations capture only fragments of an already existing reality.

### AI reconstructs educational reality.

Rejected because AI can only assist awareness, never replace reality itself.
