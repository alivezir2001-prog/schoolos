# Boundary Review

The purpose of Boundary Review is to ensure that every theoretical layer within SchoolOS has a unique responsibility.

Each theory must clearly define:

- what it owns,
- what it produces,
- what it receives,
- and what it must never do.

If two theories own the same responsibility, the architecture becomes ambiguous.

If one theory performs another theory's responsibility, explainability is lost.

SchoolOS therefore defines explicit boundaries for every theoretical layer.

---

# Educational Reality

## Responsibility

Define the ontological foundation of educational reality.

## Owns

- Educational Reality
- Existence
- Educational change
- Human development

## Never Owns

Educational Reality never:

- captures observations
- reveals patterns
- generates evidence
- creates meaning
- supports decisions

## Receives

Nothing.

Educational Reality exists independently.

## Produces

Educational Reality

## Success Criterion

Reality exists independently of observation.

## Failure Condition

Reality becomes dependent on observers or technology.

# Observation Theory

## Responsibility

Capture authentic fragments of educational reality.

## Owns

- Observations
- Observer
- Context
- Time
- Historical preservation

## Never Owns

Observation must never:

- interpret
- explain
- classify
- diagnose
- predict
- generate meaning
- justify trust
- estimate confidence
- recommend action

## Receives

Educational Reality

## Produces

Observations

## Success Criterion

Reality is faithfully preserved without interpretation.

## Failure Condition

Observation contains interpretation, judgement or explanation.

# Pattern Theory

## Responsibility

Reveal recurring educational structures.

## Owns

- Patterns
- Educational Awareness
- Structural emergence
- Recurrence

## Never Owns

Pattern must never:

- generate meaning
- justify trust
- estimate confidence
- diagnose learners
- recommend interventions
- replace professional judgement

## Receives

Observations

## Produces

Patterns

## Success Criterion

Previously hidden educational structures become progressively visible.

## Failure Condition

Patterns are created through superficial similarity, labels or assumptions instead of recurring educational structures.

# Evidence Theory

## Responsibility

Justify why a Pattern deserves educational trust.

## Owns

- Evidence
- Observable justification
- Traceability
- Explainability

## Never Owns

Evidence must never:

- interpret meaning
- estimate confidence
- diagnose learners
- recommend actions
- replace educational judgement

## Receives

Patterns

Observations

## Produces

Evidence

## Success Criterion

Every Pattern can be transparently justified through observable educational support.

## Failure Condition

Evidence cannot explain why a Pattern deserves trust.

Support responsible educational action.
