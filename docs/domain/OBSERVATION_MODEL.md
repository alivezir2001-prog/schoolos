# Observation Model

> **An Observation is a human-recorded educational event that preserves a fragment of reality within its educational context.**

The Observation Model defines one of the fundamental domain entities of SchoolOS.

Observation is the entry point of the Understanding Engine.

It is the smallest unit of educational reality that can be intentionally preserved.

Observations are not conclusions.

They are historical records.

---

# Purpose

The purpose of an Observation is to preserve educational reality without interpretation.

Observations allow understanding to emerge gradually through accumulation rather than immediate judgement.

---

# Domain Responsibility

Observation is responsible for:

- preserving reality
- preserving context
- preserving time
- preserving authorship

Observation is **not** responsible for:

- interpretation
- assessment
- classification
- prediction
- educational decisions

These responsibilities belong to later stages of the Knowledge Flow.

---

# Observation Identity

Every Observation represents a unique educational event.

An Observation always belongs to:

- one learner
- one observer
- one educational context
- one moment in time

---

# Educational Context

An Observation never exists in isolation.

Its context includes:

- School
- Academic Year
- Teaching Assignment
- Observer
- Learner
- Time
- Optional Location

Without context, an observation loses much of its educational meaning.

# Observation Lifecycle

```
Reality

↓

Human Attention

↓

Observation Created

↓

Stored

↓

Pattern Recognition

↓

↓

```

Observation itself never becomes Evidence.

It remains a permanent historical record.

---

# Observation Principles

## Human Created

Observations can only be created by people.

Artificial Intelligence never creates observations.

---

## Contextual

Every observation belongs to an educational context.

Context is inseparable from meaning.

---

## Neutral

Observations should describe what was noticed.

They should avoid interpretation whenever possible.

Good:

> "The learner volunteered to explain the solution."

Poor:

> "The learner has excellent leadership skills."

---

## Historical

An observation records something that happened.

It is a historical event.

---

## Immutable

Observation content should not be rewritten after creation.

Minor corrections may be allowed.

Educational meaning should never be changed afterwards.

If an observation becomes invalid, it should be marked accordingly rather than rewritten.

---

## Independent

Each observation is independent.

Relationships emerge later through Pattern Recognition.

Observations should never reference future interpretations.

---

# Domain Rules

## Rule 1

Only authenticated human users may create observations.

---

## Rule 2

Every observation must belong to exactly one learner.

---

## Rule 3

Every observation must identify its observer.

---

## Rule 4

Every observation must have an observation time.

---

## Rule 5

Every observation must belong to an educational context.

The educational context is represented by a Teaching Assignment.

---

## Rule 6

Observation content should describe reality rather than conclusions.

---

## Rule 7

Observations are never automatically generated.

---

## Rule 8

Artificial Intelligence may analyse observations but never modify them.

---

## Rule 9

Deleting observations should be avoided.

Invalid observations should instead be marked as invalid while preserving history.

---

# Observation Quality

A high-quality observation is:

- factual
- concise
- contextual
- understandable
- human

Length is not a measure of quality.

One accurate sentence may be more valuable than a long report.

---

# Examples

Good

> "The learner encouraged a classmate who was hesitant to participate."

Good

> "The learner requested additional time to improve the project."

Good

> "The learner remained engaged throughout the group discussion."

Avoid

> "The learner is responsible."

Avoid

> "The learner has strong collaboration skills."

These statements belong to Insight rather than Observation.

# Position Within SchoolOS

```
Reality

↓

Observation

↓

Pattern

↓

Evidence

↓

Insight

↓

Understanding
```

Observation is the foundation upon which every later stage depends.

Its responsibility is not to explain reality.

Its responsibility is to preserve it.

---

# Core Philosophy

> **Capture reality.**

> **Preserve context.**

> **Avoid conclusions.**

> **Trust accumulation.**

# Future Evolution

Future versions of the Observation Model may support:

- Multiple observers
- Observation visibility levels
- Observation verification
- Collaborative observations
- Voice observations
- Mobile offline capture

These capabilities should never compromise the core principles of the Observation Model.
